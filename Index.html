Sergeant Major Avery Johnson

United Nations Space Command

Sergeant Major Avery Junior Johnson was a senior non-commissioned officer of the UNSC Marine Corps who served during the Insurrection and the Human-Covenant War. Johnson was a highly trained Marine, serving with various units of the Naval Special Warfare Command, most notably the highly classified ORION Project, the UNSC's first supersoldier program.

Johnson played a vital role during the course of the Covenant War. He resisted the Covenant's invasion of Harvest at the beginning of the war and went on to serve in numerous engagements. In the war's final months Johnson fought at Reach, and the events at Alpha Halo, Earth, Delta Halo, and the Ark.

He was key in forming an alliance between humanity and the Sangheili following the Great Schism and the outbreak of the Flood in November 2552. Ultimately, Sergeant Major Johnson was killed in an attempt to prematurely activate a replacement Halo by monitor 343 Guilty Spark. He was honored by Fleet Admiral Lord Terrence Hood twice, first with the Colonial Cross just prior to the invasion of Earth, then at the memorial in Voi, Kenya, following the end of the war.
